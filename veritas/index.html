Veritas

The Principia engine applied to investigative journalism and public claims

The Proposition

The same adversarial logic that stress-tests a physics theory can be applied to a news story, a government report, or a corporate disclosure. Claims can be decomposed. Evidence gaps can be named. Verification can be opened to bounty.

Principia Veritas extends the engine beyond science into the domain of public epistemic integrity — where the cost of unexamined claims is not a failed publication but a failed democracy.

The Architecture

A submitted investigation is parsed into its constituent claims. Each claim is classified: demonstrated, asserted, or unverified. The engine identifies the highest-value gaps — the unverified claims on which the story’s most consequential conclusions depend — and posts them as open verification bounties.

Journalists, researchers, and institutions who supply the required documentation close the bounty and are credited in the public record.

Status

Principia Veritas is in the design phase. The underlying engine is proven. Platform architecture, editorial governance, and funding structure are under active development.
