The Process

A theory submitted to the Principia engine passes through six independent analytical agents in sequence. Each agent interrogates the work from a distinct epistemic posture — probing assumptions, demanding evidence, constructing refutations, and stress-testing logical architecture.

No agent knows the conclusions of the others until synthesis. Agreement earns no weight; only survived falsification does.

The Six Agents

Advocatvs Diaboli

Constructs the strongest possible case against the submitted theory. Seeks logical contradictions, empirical gaps, and unacknowledged assumptions.

Entropy

Prevents premature convergence. Introduces perturbations, alternative framings, and edge cases that other agents may have dismissed too quickly.

Validatio

Audits every empirical claim against observable data and prior literature. Distinguishes demonstrated from merely consistent.

Synthesis

Integrates surviving claims into a coherent formal structure. Identifies what has been genuinely established versus what remains conjecture.

Acervvs

The archivist. Maintains institutional memory across the analysis — flags internal inconsistencies, tracks evolving claims, records what was conceded under pressure.

Confvtatio

The steel-maner. Reconstructs the theory in its strongest possible form before final evaluation, ensuring critique is directed at the best version of the argument.
